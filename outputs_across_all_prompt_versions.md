## Key Observations Across Versions

**Version 1 → Version 2:** Adding role and constraints improved tone 
and reduced the most generic filler language. However, [Organization Name] 
and [Your Name] placeholders persisted throughout all five cases. Case 3 
(unknown donor) still failed to produce an opener — the email started 
mid-sentence with no salutation. Case 5 was overly apologetic with no 
acknowledgment of the underlying tension with Mark Schwartz.

**Version 2 → Version 3:** Adding organizational identity and sender 
persona eliminated all generic placeholders across all five cases. Outputs 
began referencing Adventist HealthCare, Maryland, and specific program 
areas by name. Case 3 now fills in AHC pediatric context rather than 
leaving blanks. Case 4 handles the sensitive named gift conversation 
without inventing details about Dr. Chung's husband. Case 5 shifted 
from apologetic to professionally candid. Every email now closes with 
a real signature block, making outputs genuinely closer to sendable drafts.
