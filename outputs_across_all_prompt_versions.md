## Key Observations Across Versions

**Version 1 → Version 2:** Adding role and constraints improved tone 
and reduced the most generic filler language. However, [Organization Name] 
and [Your Name] placeholders persisted throughout, the ask amount was 
correct but impact language remained vague, and Case 5 was overly 
apologetic — the tension with Mark Schwartz was acknowledged but 
immediately smoothed over rather than addressed directly.

**Version 2 → Version 3:** Adding organizational identity and sender 
persona eliminated all generic placeholders. Outputs began referencing 
Adventist HealthCare, Maryland, and specific program areas by name. 
Case 5 shifted from apologetic to professionally candid — acknowledging 
the difficult experience without groveling. Subject lines became 
purposeful. The signature block reinforced institutional credibility 
and gave the output the feel of a real, sendable letter.
